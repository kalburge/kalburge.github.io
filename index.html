Ishan Kalburge

I am a third-year undergraduate at Johns Hopkins University majoring in Biomedical Engineering, Applied Mathematics and Statistics, and Economics. My research interests lie at the intersection of computational decision neuroscience, statistical theory, and behavioral economics.
I conduct research with Dr. Vikram Chib @ Hopkins on interoception in motor control and with Dr. Colin Camerer @ Caltech on computational models of bursty human behavior. I am an incoming researcher at the Gold Lab @ Penn, where I am leading a project to study how bursty human behavior arises from multi-armed bandit models of normative decision rules in dynamic environments.
