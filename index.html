I am a third-year undergraduate at Johns Hopkins University majoring in Biomedical Engineering (Biomedical Data Science), Applied Mathematics and Statistics (Probability and Stochastic Processes), and Economics. My research interests lie at the intersection of computational decision neuroscience, applied statistics and machine learning, and behavioral and neuroeconomics. At the Hopkins School of Medicine, I conduct research with Dr. Vikram Chib on motor control and complex decision-making in physical effort tasks. I am an incoming researcher at the Gold Lab @ the Perelman School of Medicine and research fellow in the Camerer Group @ Caltech Center for Social and Decision Neuroscience .
